# House Harlequin — Creative Redesign Brief

## The Shift

FROM: Jewelry catalog with placeholder patterns
TO: A creative director's private mood board — behind-the-scenes brand world

## Feeling

Like finding Cruella de Vil's sketchbook in a locked drawer.
Dark paper. Ink. Gold leaf. Torn edges. Pinned photographs.
Not a website. A document that wasn't meant to be found.

## Image Strategy

Images are THE site. Not decoration — they ARE the content.
- Full-bleed, edge-to-edge, overlapping, cropped aggressively
- Dark-dominant: every image should live in shadow
- Mix photography with illustration/sketch energy
- No jewelry product shots. Mood only.

Categories needed (user is curating):
1. ATMOSPHERE — dark fashion editorial, dramatic shadows, silhouettes
2. IDENTITY — venetian masks, masquerade, concealment
3. TEXTURE — dark velvet, gold leaf, silk, raw materials
4. PROCESS — hands crafting, atelier, tools, sketches on paper
5. SKETCHES — fashion illustrations, ink drawings, Cruella energy

## New Site Structure

### 1. ENTRANCE (replaces Hero)
- Full-viewport dark image (atmosphere category)
- Brand name barely visible — etched into the darkness
- No tagline on load. It types itself after 2 seconds: "The mask reveals. The jewel remains."
- No CTA button. Scroll is the only action.

### 2. THE CONVICTION (replaces Manifesto)
- Split screen: left = full-height mood image, right = brand manifesto text
- Text is sparse, large, italic. Not a paragraph — individual statements.
- "We don't make jewelry. We make masks for the body."
- "Every piece is a secret worn in plain sight."
- Feels like reading someone's private notes

### 3. THE MOOD BOARD (replaces House Code — THE KEY SECTION)
- Irregular grid of images — not a clean 3-column. Overlapping, different sizes.
- Some images have handwritten-style captions (CSS rotated, serif italic)
- Images from all categories mixed together
- This should feel like a wall in a creative studio — pinned, layered, alive
- Scroll-triggered: images fade/slide in from different directions
- 6-9 images in an asymmetric masonry-style layout

### 4. THE SKETCHES (replaces Collection)
- Dark background
- Fashion sketch images displayed like pages from a sketchbook
- Each "sketch" has a piece name and a single evocative line
- Vesper: "A pendant that falls like a whispered threat."
- Cipher: "Three rings. One question. No answer."
- Veil: "Earrings that move like smoke leaving a room."
- NOT product cards. More like torn pages from a journal.

### 5. THE ATELIER (replaces World)
- Behind-the-scenes energy
- One large atmospheric image of hands/workspace
- Overlaid with sparse text about process
- "We work in a room with no mirrors. The piece tells us when it's finished."
- Feels intimate, private, not-for-public

### 6. ENQUIRE (stays similar but elevated)
- "This is not a store. There is no cart."
- "If a piece speaks to you, we should speak to each other."
- Email field. Nothing else.
- The most minimal section on the page.

### 7. FOOTER
- Barely there. Brand name. Year. Nothing else.

## Typography Adjustments
- Display text should be MUCH larger — 8-12vw for hero moments
- Body text stays sparse. Never more than 2 sentences together.
- Handwritten-style captions on mood board (CSS transform: rotate(-2deg))

## Color Adjustments
- Less ivory, more black. The light sections should be dark cream at most.
- Champagne used only for accent moments — less frequent = more precious
- Introduce a warm amber/gold for image overlays

## Layout Philosophy
- Break the grid. Not everything centered. Not everything symmetrical.
- Images bleed to edges. Text floats over images.
- Generous negative space between text sections, but images can be dense/overlapping.
- The site should feel VERTICAL — tall, narrow reading column for text, full-bleed for images.

## Copy Tone
- First person plural ("we") but secretive, not corporate
- Short declarative sentences. No fluff.
- Reads like notes to self, not marketing copy
- Occasional questions that aren't answered
- "Why gold? Because it remembers every hand that touches it."
